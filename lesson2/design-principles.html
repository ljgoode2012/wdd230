Visual Hierarchy

Tynker

Tynker.com

With a large white heading that is significantly larger than any other text, your eyes immediately read Tynker's most important message. Their product is easy coding skills development for kids. Next, the bright orange button pulls your attention, offering you an action to take, with the message that it is free to try. Finally, the small text in conjunction with the image of the game, sends the message that the product is fun to play. Using the design principle of visual hierarchy, Tynker effectively takes your eyes through a journey that informs you their product is easy, free, and fun.

Hick's Law

Chase

Chase.com

The Chase website opens with a clean, simple menu of options. The bank offers such a variety of services and products, many of which the average user may not understand. Without good organization it would be easy to get lost in them. Using Hick's law, the designers have simplified Chase's services and products into categories that are easy to understand with icons that help the categories become more user friendly.

White Space

Progressive

Progressive.com

Although the term "white space" in web design doesn't have to actually be the color white, Progressive does a particularly good job of using white to create a unique clean experience for their customers. On their website they use the white space to streamline the content and allow your eyes to focus on one thing at a time. This creates a very clean and easy reading and scrolling experience. It also allows their logo and brand to have a very strong presence without being overshadowed or muted by other messages.
